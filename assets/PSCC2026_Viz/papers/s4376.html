Insights from Practice: How to conduct large-scale dynamic simulations of low-inertia power systems?

As power system dynamics are increasingly dominated by comparatively many CIDERs, detailed system\-level stability studies have become a central part in stability assessment, but remain difficult due to limitations in today’s simulation tools and the complexity of assembling credible models. Building on experience modelling parts of the Danish and Australian power systems, this talk focuses on the practical workflow and decision-making required to conduct large‑scale time-domain studies: obtaining and parameterizing models, combining different model types, handling missing data, and navigating the computational limits of EMT, RMS, and hybrid simulators. Motivated by these real‑world challenges, the session further discusses the use of a range of dynamic model‑reduction approaches, including classical, data‑driven, and ML‑based methods. The talk concludes with open research questions and emerging needs for future grids targeting high or fully non‑synchronous operation.
